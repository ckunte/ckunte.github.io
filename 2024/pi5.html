11 Oct 2024

The daily driver

The mail order placed last week arrived on Monday. The box contained (a) Raspberry Pi 5 + official power adapter, (b) official active cooler, and (c) micro HDMI adapter. Assembly was straightforward; I paid attention to installing the active cooler. Next up was software.

The Raspberry Pi OS Desktop also comes pre-installed with Chromium browser and Evince document viewer, which are both excellent. I also installed Gimp (and considered installing Inkscape). It’s what I need to be productive.

I like the portability of this Pi; conditions me to sit at a desk, not be crouched in an awkward, neck-loading posture with a laptop. It goes into my pack when I want to use it at work, where I have a KVM set-up.

I’ve been running this for a few days now. It needs the top cover-off to avoid increasing board temperature. The custom USB-C power adapter (with 5A/5V requirement) is an oddity. Will soon be adding the official SSD via M.2 HAT, which I expect will make this a little faster.
