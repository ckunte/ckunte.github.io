8 Apr 2024

Move again

We moved seven times (or nine, if I count the two assignments that did not allow me until my kids finish school-year) in the last 17+ years. I was 34 then, and my kids were 6 and 2. Leaving school at one location and rejoining at another; packing personal effects at one, and unpacking at another; cleaning out and leaving the rental accommodation at one, and search for one at another; selling or giving-away stuff unfit for the voyage, and re-purchase at destination; living out of suitcases in hotels or service apartments while the cargo was at sea, incurring losses from buying + selling, et al.

By my sixth assignment, body and mind were both scrambling into self-preservation mode, and screaming at me to get off this wheel. So last October, I returned home and told my employer that I was struggling, and that I could no longer do this. By end-January, post handover + other formalities, we parted ways. The many responses I received from the community of engineers, I have known for years, was overwhelming as they expressed shock, regret, wishing best, and hopes of working together in future again. My reason to leave was met with empathy, outrage, but mostly one of understanding. It was a humbling experience.

Since then, I’ve been on a short sabbatical. We are now preparing to move back to AU to be closer to my university-going daughter, and to a job there. April, for my wife and I, has been a slow grind in preparing for yet another move. My stomach is already in a twist.
