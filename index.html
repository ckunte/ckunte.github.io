24W25: Shoebox

Allied’s service was good. Their promise was 7-9 weeks; crating some part of their service. The container arrived at my doorstep in 8. Stuff intact, bar one. They reminded us to get transit bolts to protect the washer, which we did just in time.

We fixed (a) the dining table, (b) dining sideboard and bookcase shutters, and (c) the shared work desk and vesa mount. We finally found the shoebox — containing useful bits and bobs, including my portable speaker.

Years in the making, numpy 2.0 landed this week. I got to test my scripts written over the years with the following; they’re still usable.

grep "numpy" *.py | ruff check --select NPY201

24W24: Unpacking

We got back the container mid-week. Unloading done by mid-day, but unpacking is still underway. Furniture to re-assemble and stuff to re-arrange. Except for an old speaker that refuses to power-up, nothing else seems broken, but we are missing little things.

Every time I look at our stuff, I want to throw-up. I think we have got too much that’s just not visible until it comes out of a container. We end up carrying it all is because we disagree on how much we have, and I am not in control.

24W22: Two Typst packages

Tired of repeating blocks while writing documents in Typst, I developed two packages: typst-snippets-st for Sublime Text, and typst-snippets-vim for Vim. They are a work-in-progress. I am using the first as I have no access to linux environment at work.

My request for WSL on work laptop was denied on security grounds. Classic gate-keeping. OS makers are the worst. What’s in user space should never be behind Admin; yet here we are.

We moved into the house a couple of weeks ago. The cargo arrived into Fremantle last week but yet to arrive at our doorstep; hopefully next week.
